The world of Evrenthall is divided into nine main landmasses, considered to be the **Nine Continents**. Each of them was shaped by one of the [[Pantheon Intro#Founder Amphytrion|Founder Amphytrion]], giving it unique climatic, social and cultural characteristics setting it aside from the others.
Each continent is associated with various symbols, generally unique and clearly distinguishable from those of the other continents: these include the respective coats of arms, the glyph associated with the continent name (pictured before it in most cases) or the title bestowed upon it, among other minor ones.

# Introduction to the Continents
## ༺ Eshayunad, the Viridian Cradle

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_eshayunad.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Eshayunad

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> May you always find verdant paths to follow.
> 
> 💬 A common Eshayunan blessing

With a surface covered in lush forests and gentle elevations, rivers and streams flowing abundant and crystalline, and general cultural practices among the most open and welcoming in all of Evrenthall, [[Eshayunad]] is a place of peace and delight above all others, possibly the closest someone will ever experience to visiting [[Euneria]] for as long as they'd like, still awake and aware.

Above anyone else, in any faithful Eshayunan's mind, is the Emperor, personally chosen by [[Ydani the Viridian]] through his vessel on Evrenthall—the previous Emperor, who chooses their successor whenever Ydani whispers to them that the time to leave their place and rejoin Nature is drawing closer—and their [[Imperial Praetorians|Praetorians]], the guards following them everywhere, from the throne to the grave; next come the Clerics and Priests of Ydani, the Servants of the Floral Lords, and many other religious figures. It is a place of deep spirituality and connection to life—best symbolized by their belief, almost completely unique across Evrenthall, in a total reunification with Nature upon death.

## 𓎞 Hysthyalm, the Wayward Compass

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_hysthyalm.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Hysthyalm

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> You can't make a good golem from bad copper.
> 
> 💬 A common Hysthyalmic saying

Profound [[Magic Intro|Fluxes of Magic]] course through [[Hysthyalm]], in every drop of water of its rivers and every little stone of its mountains and every tiny spark of its fires and every faint gust of air of its winds, shaped by both the many deities who took interest in this place and by the Perturbation engulfing it changing all it comes into contact.

What is unknown about Hysthyalm greatly surpasses what *is* known about it: great mysteries await those with eyes to search for them, those who are ready to plunge into the depths of reality to uncover its deepest secrets, guarded by constructed creatures neither living nor lifeless.  
Many powerful Mages from this continent long to elevate themselves to the status of deities through the creation of a new life—a *real* life, with its own mind and its own identity—from inert matter, following the footsteps of their Founder, [[Mydorahm the Guardian]], and such acts often do not go unnoticed, nor does the hubris in them go unpunished. Only the most ancient guardians, created with Mydorahm's direct help, still persist in their task, eternal and undying; most of the others, along with their creators, encountered the harshest of punishment, and even of their memory remains no trace.

## 🝳 Luerien, the Abyssal Lantern

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_luerien.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Luerien


> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> What is it *really* that differentiates the depths of the sea and the heights of the sky?
> 
> 💬 *Elements of the Philosophy of the Depths*, Ithiel Theodrade

A ring encircling an expanse of saltwater, with wonders hidden both below, among the waves of the sea, and above, between the clouds of the sky: such is how [[Luerien]] is most often described, allowing a glimpse into the wonders the continent has to offer. 

Divided, yet in many ways (although not always knowingly) united, many of its Denizens—whether they inhabit the Floating Islands of Silaneaul, the three countries of the Ring or the depths of the Casmar Trench—live in prosperity, peace and harmony, often too invested in the frivolous lifestyle they can enjoy to really worry about whatever tragedies may befall the world around them.  
The ruling classes and families are almost completely opposite to this jovial attitude, having many more concerns and many more aspects of life to manage, and generally prefer to spend their free time engaging in arts, philosophy and music rather than in parties and festivals, in turn providing Luerien with one of the richest cultures of all Evrenthall. When they do participate in the celebrations, it is often to conceal what truly worries them; someday, the citizens are bound to notice that not everything is as wonderful as it seems, and the reaction could be devastating.

## ⶑ Maaghteab, the Firstborn Heart


> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_maaghteab.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Maaghteab

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> Know thyself, and nothing shall be unknown.
> 
> 💬 Inscription on the doors of Tyrar

According to the traditional history of Evrenthall, [[Maaghteab]] was the first of the continents to come into existence, and the one where [[Denizens]] first made their appearance. Its history, its culture, its very roots, all of these aspects are the most ancient in Evrenthall when only considering the things not having its origins from the Worlds Before, and the Denizens of this continent are often so in touch with their past that they can sometimes catch glimpses of their future in the most fleeting moments of their lives: be they a reflection on a mirror, a leaf falling in the wind or a spark elevating from a fire, it is the little things that truly matter.

Above all, Maaghteab is a place of light, shining in its every aspect, from the grandiose buildings of white marble and gold to the fires always lit at the center of many of its city-states, serving as beacons of hope and as reminders of the divine favor that chose to make Maaghteab as its first creation.

## ⪀ Qor'chkis, the Glacial Fortress

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_qorchkis.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Qor'chkis

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> A piece of gold won't save you from a hungry owlbear.
> 
> 💬 A common Qorchkiskan saying

The reputation of [[Qor'chkis]] precedes it: never once kind to people not born among its lands, inhospitable as few places are, most often deadly for its inhabitants and foreigners alike. There are very few Qorchkiskans whose one priority in life is to make it through another day, to survive even just until the next sunrise, and the ones who don't concern themselves with such thoughts are considered either completely mad or way too privileged for their own good—what would happen if, for some reason, the institutions protecting them were to collapse, abandoning them to the long, freezing winters of the continent?

While individualism is still preferred by some, who believe relying only on one's self is the only way not to get dragged into a dangerous situation by a companion's recklessness, many have understood the significance of community, of helping out one another in a place where you can never know if you will wake up tomorrow, of keeping resilient in the face of hardships thanks to the closeness of a friend. If anything can help you not to die, it's valuable; the same thing goes for *anyone* who can help you not to die.

## ⌯ Tellurväuhr, the Last Frontier

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_tellurvauhr.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Tellurväuhr

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> The gears will keep on turning anyway.
> 
> 💬 A common Tellvaihdian saying

In common speech, [[Tellurväuhr]] is pretty much synonymous with progress, and for a good reason: of all the continents, it is the one where industrialization has reached its highest point, with technologies (both purely mechanical and technomagical) almost never seen anywhere else in Evrenthall being quite a common sight even in everyday life.

The old days of glory of the Empire have been gone for a good while, and only a memory of the ancient colonies still persists; still, Tellurians take incredible pride into what they have become, what they have shaped themselves to be against all odds.

## ꁨ Xiu-Renyou, the Visionary Chain

> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_xiurenyou.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Xiu-Renyou

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> There is no such thing as an uncontrollable event.
> 
> 💬 One of the tenets of the Renyou Unified Government

Of all the continents, [[Xiu-Renyou]] is the smallest and most isolated one, only engaging in very sporadic communication with the outside world when it is strictly necessary. Besides, what could the world have to give to a country-continent that already runs as it should, what could external input do except for shaking the foundations of what Xiu-Renyou *is*? Of course, getting to this point required some past *intervention*, but now it is all as it was always meant to be. *Always.*



## ꙮ Yaefrem, the Sunlit Crown


> [!caption|left]
> ![[z_attachments/armoria/armoria_yaefrem.png|250]]
> Coat of arms of Yaefrem

> [!quote|no-t]
> 
> When you reach the top of the world, keep climbing.
> 
> 💬 A common Yaefremite saying

In a single word, the Commonwealth of [[Yaefrem]] can be—and often is—identified with one word: *supremacy*. Always priding itself its flagship trade, that of high-quality Magic Items, the Commonwealth is one of the leading economic powers in Evrenthall, surpassing even much more industrially developed areas such as Tellurväuhr, and anyone living there can easily attest to the high quality of life they have the privilege of enjoying.

Despite its facade of perfection, Yaefrem hides many tragedies in its history, ranging from natural disasters to the premature downfall of an entire royal dynasty; still, like a phoenix, Yaefremites were always able to rise from the ashes of what once was, building something new from the little that was left.
