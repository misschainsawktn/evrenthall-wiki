**Pain** is but a part of life. As much as that notion may be detestable to some, it is true. Everyone, *everything* suffers, in one way or another—just as much as living creatures suffer when hurt or sick or dying, a stone eroded by a river suffers, a drop of water evaporating suffers, a star turning to supernova suffers, a memory being forgotten suffers. It's a harsh, yet necessary reality. It is fundamental to understanding that pain isn't good or bad in itself—like any mere tool, perfectly neutral by itself, it's the usage that is made of it that defines it.  
As far as it concerns not its usage, but its origins, it finds them in a singular entity, the **Mother of All Pain**, one of the many [[Pantheon Intro#The Hegemon, God-Concepts|Hegemon]] forming the structure of the Universe, made of the Essence of Pain. 

# Worship

Few pray to the Mother of All Pain, for a number of reasons. 

First, not all are ready to accept that pain—any form of pain, including their own—is a neutral thing. Most think of pain as an evil to be avoided at all costs, others only seek the perverse pleasure that comes with inflicting pain onto people, some others try their best to steer clear from it while submitting to the notion that it is inevitable; few embrace pain as it is and find the strength to use it as a tool, and they are not always aware that they have caught her interest in some way.

That is another one of the reasons her cult is somewhat limited: the Great Mother is very selective when it comes to her followers, and she is not above picking and choosing the ones she finds most interesting among those who suffer. For someone to be initiated, she must like them enough to want them at her side, and she does not make exceptions to this rule

# Manifestations

As most Hegemon do, the Mother of All Pain cannot show herself to anyone at the full potential of her power, lest whoever witnesses her die; however, she chooses to take on different forms depending on who is the one beholding her, basing her judgement on their belonging to her cult or not, as a safety precaution.

Manifestations of the Mother of All Pain are known to cause, in those who are not used to her presence, symptoms such as headaches, nausea, dizziness, blurry vision, cramps and, in extreme cases, an urge to self-harm in some way. The severity of said symptoms depends on both how much Essence the Manifestation has and on how used a being is to her presence near them.
## Non-Believers

![[z_attachments/full_char_art/mother_of_all_pain_nonbelievers.jpg|hm-sm ws-med cover p+cct right]]

To those not a part of her cult (and to the initiates still not used to her presence), the Mother of All Pain shows a limited form, concealing most of her features and of her power behind layers of cloth and a mask.  

In this Manifestation, she appears almost human—keyword is *almost*, as she is remains clearly *something else entirely*—and lacks many of the characteristics only an acolyte gets to see: her colors are much duller and human-like, her clothing less elaborate, although still elegant, her face (if you can call it that) is never once shown unless strictly necessary, much less the body hiding beneath the fabric of her dress.  
What still shows is her arms, her arms and her *many* hands, sprawling from under her veils, with no clear origin nor any order in how they're placed, all perfectly independent from one another. Only two of the hands seem to be attached to "real" arms, starting from the shoulders as is normal for almost any Denizen, while the others appear to arbitrarily stop at the wrist or at the elbow, in a pile of fingers and joints with no rhyme or reason—as those who have witnessed her other form may testify, that couldn't be farther from the truth, but not all are ready to see that.

The Essence put in this Manifestation is incredibly limited, and most of it is contained thanks to some expedients the Mother of All Pain uses. Some say that her mask is the key, and that if she were to remove it, the sight could kill a person; others claim that the issue lies in what her clothes conceal, both because of the many hands and arms making their way out from under them and because of the heartbeat one can hear when too close. A heartbeat that's not their own, one that isn't even a singular heartbeat, but many superimposed over one another.

## Acolytes

> [!cite|subtitle] "UN CASTELLO Pt. 3" _Ozone Dehumanizer_
> *Forse il dolore è una forma d'amore*  
> *Forse l'amore è un eterno dolore*

![[z_attachments/full_char_art/mother_of_all_pain_acolytes.jpeg|hm-sm ws-med cover right]]


The most loyal followers of the Mother of All Pain, those who offered to fully devote themselves to her and saw their request accepted, get to behold a much more powerful Manifestation, much closer to the real nature of their Deity and, in their eyes, much more beautiful.

The Great Mother's colors shine bright in this Manifestation, ever-changing and pure, and she no longer hides many parts of her body: while a veil still covers her face, one can see teeth and eyes poking through, and her cape no longer covers the many arms, turning into centipede legs, and her body, an elongated mass of flesh, pulsing and alive, nor does it hide the many hearts embedded in it.  
That is, possibly, one of the most identifying features of the Mother of All Pain, those living, beating hearts finding their place in their own holes open in the Mother's body—hearts that once belonged to her Acolytes, and that she now always keeps with herself, *within* herself, as a pledge of devotion from her loving and beloved followers. Of course, she would never leave them completely hollow, as she greatly values their survival: to fill the gaping wound in their chest, she provides them with a core of [[Magic Intro|Magic]] and [[Essence]], capable of keeping the receiver alive even if their heart is gone.

The Essence of Pain in this Manifestation is still limited, yet enormous if compared to what a non-believer might see, enough that it could be, in many ways, extremely dangerous (sometimes fatal) to someone not already used to her proximity.
