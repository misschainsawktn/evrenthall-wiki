# Introduction to Magic

> [!caption|right]
> ![[z_attachments/page_art/da_wizard.jpg|hm-sm cover]]
> A [[Peshak|Peshakin]] Wizard

**Magic** is a central part of most people’s lives in Evrenthall, woven into the fabric of Existence itself thanks to its connection to the [[Pantheon Intro]]. Most adventurers in Evrenthall know how to use at least a little bit of magic (although it is not a requirement to go adventuring, it comes quite handy in many situations), or use some sort of magic item, but even a lot of common people have some very basic knowledge of it—mostly in the measure where it could be helpful in their daily lives.

## Origins

As it is commonly understood, magic originates from **Fluxes of Magic**, which in turn are created by the deities of Evrenthall in various ways; said Fluxes can then be channeled and used by magic users in order to cast spells of various effects and magnitude.

Depending on the type of deity, a Flux could be generated in various ways: most Amphytrionic Fluxes come from the will of the Amphytrion making them, while Hegemonic Fluxes usually come from the movements of the corresponding Hegemon inside the fabric of Existence.

As a rule of thumb, Hegemonic Fluxes are pretty much always more powerful than Amphytrionic Fluxes, even relatively weak ones. Fluxes coming from Pseudo-Gods wildly vary in intensity, but hardly reach Hegemonic levels, usually being comparable to powerful Amphytrionic Fluxes at best.

## Learning Magic

There is no such thing as a natural-born magic user in Evrenthall: that of magic is a field of study not unlike any other, and as such must be learned in order to understand and use it. Some creatures may be bestowed with the ability to use magic by a deity, but it is not a particularly common occurrence, and even then the limits on its usage are pretty strict without additional study and training.

Most people get taught some form of simple magic since childhood—usually some inoffensive cantrips or some entry-level spells—and, later on in their life, choose whether to continue their studies in magic and, eventually, in what to specialize.

## Using Magic

Casting *any* spell requires a way to channel the Fluxes generated by a divine entity (whether it is a true deity or a pseudo-god doesn't make much of a difference) through one of the Branches of Magic (*detailed below*), but not all spells can be cast in the same exact way.

Proper spells (cantrips are excluded from this numbering system) are divided into nine “levels”, which represent both how powerful they are and how difficult they are to cast; while most spells can be efficiently cast through any form of Flux, starting from a certain level (usually the 7th, plus some 6th level ones) Amphytrionic and Pseudo-Divine Fluxes stop being strong enough to properly fuel the power of the spells, requiring a magician who wishes to use them to draw from Hegemonic Fluxes, with all the potential side effects it may bring.

Magic usage in itself can get *incredibly* specialized—there is a “family” of spell for pretty much anything, and some spellcasters enjoy the security that comes with being an expert at one thing and knowing how to execute a small amount of spells to near-perfection. Most adventuring magicians, however, have a preference for keeping their options open to a larger variety of spells, making themselves more versatile and ready for anything they might come across, be it getting over an obstacle, disarming a trap or fighting a monster.

Magic users are called by many different names, depending on their specialization; generic terms include Mage, Magician and Caster, while more specific terms include Sorcerer, Wizard, Cleric and Warlock. This is by no means a comprehensive list—when it comes to nomenclature, the sky and the caster's imagination are the limit.

# Branches of Magic

The **Branches of Magic** pertain to the way the Fluxes of Magic are created and to the way the spellcaster can draw from them. They’re not necessarily bound to any class, although there are some more associated with certain specializations due to a matter of convenience—it’s much more common to see a Warlock using Dialogic Magic and a Druid using Singular Magic, for example. Some spellcasters elect to specialize in a Branch and one Branch only, while others have a preference for choosing different ones depending on the use cases.

## Dialogic Magic

[[Dialogic Magic]] works by establishing a direct contact between the spellcaster and the being they have elected to draw their power from. Whether it is through a ritual (which can manifest through specific movements or magical formulas being pronounced), an offering of material things (worthless or expensive, depending on the spell), or any other Magic Item they can focus on in order to contact the entity, Dialogic Mages ask for a Flux with some specific features to be generated; when the connection is successful and the Flux is created correctly, the caster can use it as needed. It is considered a very cost-effective Branch of Magic, given that almost no part of the Flux goes to waste, but has its drawbacks in the necessity to first find a willing entity and the possibility of having to change the source of one's power if it stops being strong enough.

## Singular Magic

[[Singular Magic]] users find the sources of their power in the world surrounding them, without directly contacting the entity/entities generating the Fluxes, but rather by finding points where multiple Fluxes converge (the Singularity Points), stacking their base power in an additive fashion. Singularity Points are easier to locate than stray Fluxes, and their general power being higher makes them both more efficient and suitable for many different spells.

A common misconception states that Fluxes are only present in tangible things, but that is not true: music, for example, is an excellent conductor for Magic, and many notes of a melody may count as a Singularity Point.

## Chiral Magic

[[Chiral Magic]] expands on the premise of Singular Magic by making the spellcaster not rely on Fluxes from the external world through the identification of Singularity Points in one's own body, often being cast only through it or through weapons without any other mediation necessary. Given that it is a decently advanced technique and that drawing power from a living thing can have unintended side effects of varying seriousness, it is not a particularly common Branch of Magic, but the few Chiral Mages that practice it exclusively are known to be masters of their art.

## Rune Magic

> [!caption|right]
> ![[z_attachments/page_art/silaneaul_rune.jpg|175]]
> A Glyph of the Islands of Silaneaul, in [[Luerien]]

[[Rune Magic]] seeks to channel Hegemonic fluxes in a relatively easy to control way, and does so through Runes, symbols and glyphs drawn or etched onto any form of solid surface. Rune Magic is incredibly effective for casting powerful, long-lasting spells, but not all Runes are equally efficient: the more common and simpler ones have a tendency to “deform” the Hegemonic Fluxes going through them due to the Fluxes having to adapt to the shape of the Rune and to the material it's inscribed on, while more complex and refined ones can circumvent this issue at the cost of taking much longer to draw and having a lot more research behind them.

There is no singular way a Rune can look, and of all the Branches it is probably the most diverse one in how it manifests: different areas of Evrenthall have developed characteristic Rune styles to match the materials used and the cultural background of the place, and many expert Rune Mages elaborate their own personal styles during their studies.

# Schools of Magic

Rather than focusing on _how_ a spell is cast as the Branches do, the **Schools of Magic** focus on _what_ the spell being cast actually does. For convenience’s sake, all known spells have been cataloged into 8 Schools, with the addition of a 9th “improper” School that is still listed with them, the Perturbation.

(I might have copied these directly from the PHB with some edits to better fit the setting, excluding Perturbation, which is pretty much just Wild Magic anyway…)

## Abjuration

**Abjuration** spells are protective in nature, though some of them have aggressive uses. They create magical barriers, negate harmful effects, harm trespassers, or banish creatures to other Realms of Existence.

## Conjuration

**Conjuration** spells involve the transportation of objects and creatures from one location to another. Some spells summon creatures or objects to the caster's side, whereas others allow the caster to teleport to another location. Some conjurations create objects or effects out of nothing.

## Divination

**Divination** spells reveal information, whether in the form of secrets long forgotten, glimpses of the future, the locations of hidden things, the truth behind illusions, or visions of distant people or places.

## Enchantment

**Enchantment** spells affect the minds of others, influencing or controlling their behavior. Such spells can make enemies see the caster as a friend, force creatures to take a course of action, or even control another creature like a puppet.

## Evocation

**Evocation** spells directly manipulate the energy of the Fluxes to produce a desired effect. Some call up blasts of fire or lightning. Others channel positive energy to heal wounds.

## Illusion

**Illusion** spells deceive the senses or minds of others. They cause people to see things that are not there, to miss things that are there, to hear phantom noises, or to remember things that never happened. Some illusions create phantom images that any creature can see, but the most insidious illusions plant an image directly in the mind of a creature.

## Necromancy

**Necromancy** spells manipulate the energies of life and death. Such spells can grant an extra reserve of life force, drain the life energy from another creature, create the undead, or even bring the dead back to life.

## Transmutation

**Transmutation** spells change the properties of a creature, object, or environment. They might turn an enemy into a harmless creature, bolster the strength of an ally, make an object move at the caster's command, or enhance a creature's innate healing abilities to rapidly recover from injury.

## The Perturbation

**The Perturbation** is the wild, uncontrollable variation naturally present in all Fluxes of Magic—no two Fluxes are the same, even if they are generated by the same Deity in otherwise perfectly identical conditions.
